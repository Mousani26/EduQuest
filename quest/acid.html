Acids

Concept & Definitions

Acids are substances that donate protons or accept electrons. They have a pH less than 7 and turn blue litmus paper red. Common theories include Arrhenius, Brønsted-Lowry, and Lewis definitions.

Acid Formula

Real-life Applications

Acids are used in various industries and everyday life, such as in batteries, food preservation, and cleaning agents. Examples include sulfuric acid, citric acid, and hydrochloric acid.

Summary

Acids are proton donors with a pH below 7. They react with bases, metals, and carbonates. Common acids include sulfuric, citric, and hydrochloric acids.
