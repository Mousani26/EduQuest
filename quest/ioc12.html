The p-Block Elements (Group 17 & 18)

Elements in groups 17 and 18, known as halogens and noble gases, exhibit unique chemical properties due to their electron configurations.

The d-Block Elements (Transition Elements)

Transition elements, or d-block elements, are characterized by their ability to form complex ions and exhibit variable oxidation states.

The f-Block Elements (Lanthanides & Actinides)

Lanthanides and actinides, or f-block elements, are known for their radioactive properties and complex electronic structures.

Coordination Compounds

Coordination compounds involve metal ions bonded to ligands, forming complex structures with diverse applications in catalysis and materials science.

The General Principles and Processes of Isolation of Elements

The extraction and purification of elements from their natural sources involve various chemical processes, including reduction, oxidation, and electrolysis.
